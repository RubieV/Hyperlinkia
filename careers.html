Careers

With the explosive increase of data in Internet era, the data-savvy professionals are more and more desired in various kinds of fields such as scientific research, medicine, businesses and government. The supply of professionals who can work effectively with data is limited, and is reflected by rapidly rising salaries for data scientists, statisticians, and data analysts. It seems that the only problem with this career is supply and demand.

McKinsey and Company reports that by 2018, there will be 140,000-190,000 data related job postings that go unfulfilled. The authors also project a need for 1.5 million more managers and analysts with deep analytical and technical skills “who can ask the right questions and consume the results of analysis of big data effectively.” In other words, the next three years is a veritable gold mine for data scientists. Be ready for the sexiest job of 21st century!

Scientific Research

Data scientists investigate complex problems through expertise in disciplines within the fields of mathematics, statistics , and computer science. But the usage of data is not just limited in these fields. Actually, the essence of science is to systematically build and organize knowledge in the form of testable explanations and predictions about the universe.

The material we use to build and test hypothesis is data. It is necessary to know how to collect, mine, analysis and interpret data nearly in every domain of science, including economics, biological sciences, health care, social sciences and humanities etc. Therefore a data scientist not only can engage in a mathematics or statistics field but also are welcome to work as part of a team in many scientific areas in universities or research institutions.

Medicin

Medical facilities generate and process huge volumes of medical information and medical device data, and have been accelerating big data utilization. Big data is changing traditional medical research that the test done in a lab and need to wait for new data into a new fast way to get data online from searchable databases that make instant answers possible.

What is required for further advancement of medical care is analysis based on integrated data, such as clinical data and genomic analysis data, life data including SNS and other data, and data collected from home healthcare devices for remote medical care and disease management. Many famous companies such as Microsoft, Intel, IBM and Philips are dedicated to providing ever more meaningful solutions to support efficient and high quality medical care.

Business

Big data is enabling companies to gain new insights into areas ranging from customer behaviour to how their businesses function. These data-driven findings can have profound results on the bottom line. That means that products only see the light of day if they make it through rigorous statistical scrutiny. Since the firm's core product development is operating in a way that's governed by data, people in finance and business planning can't perform their functions without evaluating studies and predictions from the statisticians and data scientists.

The rarity of data scientists already has some executives describing them as “unicorns”. They believe that data scientists are more effective and bring more value to the business when they work within teams. Innovation has usually been found to occur within team environments where there are multiple skills, rather than because someone working in isolation has a brilliant idea, as often portrayed in TV dramas.

Government

Governments provide a large variety of programs and services, which both produce and require massive amounts of data, often unstructured and increasingly in real-time. This data comes from numerous sources including historical, video, cell phones, geospatial, imagery, social media, and much more. From crime prevention to transportation, health care, national security, revenue management, environmental stewardship and social services, governments must wrestle every day with managing and using this data.

There are already good examples of government using big data effectively. The troubled families initiative, for instance, has successfully combined data on families with a collaborative effort across public services at a local level. And, DJ Patil was recruited by President Obama personally and just appointed to the newly created position, where the ex-LinkedIn chief scientist will work in a supervisory role for a broad analytics effort across the federal government. His duties will include shaping policies and practices across agencies, building data science partnerships with the private sector, and helping agencies recruit talent. So if you are a data scientist, be ready to meet your president!
