Data Science

Data science is the extraction of knowledge from data. But what does that entail precisely? Data scientists use data, sets of little parts of information, to find meaningful knowledge. This is done by recognizing patterns in the data.

For example, suppose there is a set of scores of angriness. With these scores you’d able to compare male scores to female scores and you could conclude who are angrier. We wouldn’t have been able to conclude this from just individual scores; we would have had to compare them and use statistical methods.

Data science, however, is not the same as statistics, but they do have a lot in common. In data science, we are mainly interested in very big and complicated data sets: Big Data.

This Big Data is becoming increasingly more important as more and more people begin to see the benefit of using it. The amount of data increases significantly as well, and consequenly so do the possibilities of using it.

There is a lot involved in the process of data science. Data scientists therefore need a lot of skills. To be able to work with data, you will need basic mathematical and statistical and computer science knowledge.

Those are the theoretical backgrounds that every data scientist should have. For data scientists domain specific knowledge is also a necessity.

For example, a lot of data scientists work in the business sector. With just the basic knowledge of data science they won’t come far: they also need business know-how. In the bachelor of Data Science at the TU/e you are be able learn about all of these disciplines.

Mathematics & Statistics

Mathematics and statistics can be seen as the fundament of data science. In fact, statistics and data science are often used interchangeably. In both sciences, data is used to extract knowledge.

In data science however, the use of computational methods is way more extensive. This is what makes data science a very powerful tool: when the data is complicated, or there is just A LOT of data, data scientists can still work with it while pure statisticians pinch.

An engineer should never purely rely on computations. Computational programs are not flawless and data scientists should know how to detect and solve problems. For this, data scientists need mathematics and statistics.

Statistics and mathematics are hence very important areas in data science.

Computer Science

To work with data, computer science is a must. Computer science is the science of computation. Computatations are calculations with computers.

A lot of data is gathered, stored, processed and visualized by computing. Data science is thus quite heavy on computer science. Computer science in data science is a two-edged sword: it consists primarly of programming and data specific knowledge. You need programming knowledge to formalize computing methods.

Data specific knowledge is needed to determine what computing methods to use. Data scientists usually collect already existing data. This seems quite easy, but it sure isn’t. Not all data is relevant and usable. In data science, a major subject is how to collect useful data in an efficient way. This is often done with computing methods.

Acquired data should be kept somewhere. This should be done very securely. Imagine that competitors could steal data regarding a new product - this would be disastrous.

The management of data is therefore very important. Data scientists should know how to store this data on servers and alike in a professional way. When the data is collected and stored, it still has to be processed. Often, acquired data sets are complicated and big.

The data is then processed with algorithms; programs that compute in a predetermined manner. Data scientists should know how the algorithms work, as they regularly have to make their own algorithms. The knowledge is also need if they have to solve emerging problems.

After finding results, data scientists often have to present them to unknowing audiences. Next to communication, proper visualizations of data and results are then necessary.

These visualizations are often made with computers. Imagine the struggle of plotting thousands of points in a small graph. Computers are just way faster and preciser than humans in that case. In the process of data science , computer science is clearly heavily used.

Domain Specific

Data science is an applied science. It is used in business, economics, research, government… The list goes on. Skills and knowledge from these fields are really necessary to Data scientists.

Analyzing data is not enough: the results should also be interpreted. This is simply not possible without domain specific knowledge. On more positive note, the possible careers as a data scientist are diverse and pay well.

When working in different fields, co-workers, clients and bosses don’t often have a lot of data science know-how. Data scientists should then also be able to explain their work and results to others.

Communication is a very big part of this. Just like in relationships, communication is the key to success. In the elective courses of the bachelor of Data Science at the TU/e, a lot of domain specific knowledge is taught.
